Frequently Asked Questions

What does "Loam" mean?

Loam is rich soil — the kind good things grow from. In the app, each habit you tend today helps your sprout grow, a little at a time. It only ever grows; miss a day and nothing wilts. It's a calm way to see progress — you're tending growth, not defending a streak.

How do I create a habit?

Tap the + button on the Today tab. Enter a name, choose an icon and color, and set your schedule (daily, weekdays, weekends, or custom days). Tap Save and it joins today's loam right away.

How do I complete a habit?

On the Today tab, tap a habit to tend it. You'll feel a soft haptic tap as it's marked done. Changed your mind? Tap again to undo. There's no deadline and nothing is marked as failed.

How do I edit, archive, or delete a habit?

Long-press any habit on the Today tab to open the menu. Choose Edit to change it, Archive to set it aside without losing its history, or Delete to remove it and its history for good.

Can I do a habit more than once a day?

Yes. When creating or editing a habit, set Times per Day to your target (for example 8 for water or 3 for medication). Each tap counts one, and the habit is done once you reach the day's target.

What is the Ahead tab?

Ahead is your forward-looking view. Instead of grading the past, it shows your rhythm and what's coming up so you can plan a gentle nudge for tomorrow. No scores, no red days — just a calm look at the path ahead.

Does Loam track streaks or give scores?

No. Loam is deliberately grade-free. There are no streaks to break, no points, and no failure states — just today's habits and the path ahead. Miss a day and nothing is lost; you simply tend it the next time.

Is there an Apple Watch app?

Yes. Loam includes a grade-free Apple Watch app and complication. Tend a habit right from your wrist and it stays in step with your iPhone, and the complication shows how many of today's habits you've tended.

Does Loam connect to Apple Health?

Yes. When creating or editing a habit, open the Apple Health section and link it to a source (Steps, Workouts, Mindful Minutes, or Sleep). Loam completes that habit automatically once today's Health data crosses your goal. Access is read-only — Loam never writes to Apple Health and no health data ever leaves your device.

How do I set up the widget?

Long-press your iPhone Home or Lock Screen, tap the + (or Edit), search for "Loam", and add the widget. It shows today's progress at a glance.

Will Loam sync across my devices?

iCloud sync is on the roadmap. When it ships, your habits will sync privately through your own iCloud, end-to-end encrypted by Apple's CloudKit. For now, your data lives securely on your device.

How do I switch between dark and light mode?

Loam follows your iPhone's appearance by default, with a warm soil-and-slate palette tuned for both. You can also set Light or Dark directly in Settings.

Is Loam really free?

Yes. Loam is 100% free with no ads, no paywalls, and no data collection. You can optionally leave a tip ($1.99, $4.99, or $9.99) to support development, but tips don't unlock anything.

I left a tip. Can I get it back?

Tips are one-time purchases processed by Apple. For refund requests, visit reportaproblem.apple.com.

Troubleshooting

Reminders aren't arriving

Open your iPhone's Settings > Notifications > Loam and ensure notifications are enabled with Banners, Sounds, and Lock Screen turned on. Also check that a Focus mode or Do Not Disturb isn't holding them back.

Widget or Watch shows yesterday's progress

Widgets and complications refresh on the system's schedule. Open the app to tend or undo a habit and they'll catch up. If a widget stays stale, remove and re-add it.

App won't open

Force-quit the app and reopen it. If the issue persists, make sure you're on the latest version from the App Store. As a last resort, delete and reinstall the app.
